**AI Stewardship:** The discipline of deploying artificial intelligence as a human-controlled tool, with written accountability, conscience-driven oversight, and the moral infrastructure that ensures the most vulnerable people in any system are protected, not abandoned.

**The Human Infrastructure Layer:** The moral and operational foundation beneath every AI deployment — the policies, inventories, human-in-the-loop standards, and rehearsed response protocols that determine whether AI makes practitioners more present and more capable, or less.

**Shadow AI:** AI tools adopted informally by individual staff without IT or legal approval; the biggest governance gap in most organizations right now.

**Survival Infrastructure:** The governance and cybersecurity posture that determines whether a legal aid organization can survive a ransomware attack or regulatory inquiry with client trust intact.

**The Sarbanes-Oxley Moment:** The current AI governance inflection point — just as SOX made financial control ignorance legally indefensible, the convergence of ABA Opinion 512, Regulation S-P, and state AI laws is making AI governance ignorance legally indefensible for general counsel and managing partners.

**The Services Cliff:** The abrupt loss of disability services when an autistic individual ages out of IDEA protections at 21-22, entering an adult services system with no federal guarantee and waitlists measured in years.

**The Four AI Stewardship Practices:** The four observable governance disciplines — written board-ratified AI policy, documented AI inventory, human-in-the-loop standard, and rehearsed incident response playbook — that constitute the minimum viable AI Stewardship infrastructure for any legal or advocacy organization.

**Legal AI Monday:** Richard's weekly content series at LawFirmAIExpert.com providing AI adoption guidance, competitive intelligence, and governance frameworks for managing partners of small and mid-sized law firms.
